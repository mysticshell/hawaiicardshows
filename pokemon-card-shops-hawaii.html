Pokemon Card Shops in Hawaii — Sealed, Singles, Slabs, and Imports

Pokemon is the biggest TCG in Hawaii by a mile. Whether you’re hunting Japanese exclusives, cracking sealed booster boxes, or chasing a grade-10 chase card, here’s where to go on every island.

Pokemon’s hold on Hawaii is hard to overstate. It drives the biggest single-day turnouts at local shows, fills keiki goodie bags, and it’s the common language at nearly every trade night. Almost every card shop in the state carries some Pokemon — but a handful stand out for the depth of their inventory, their tournament scene, or their Japanese import selection. This is a working map of where to actually go.

How to Think About Pokemon Shopping in Hawaii

Before the shop list, a quick orientation. Most Hawaii Pokemon buyers fall into a few buckets, and the right shop depends on which one you’re in:

  • Sealed chasers — booster boxes, ETBs, special sets. Look for shops that get allocations early and carry the latest releases. ToyLynx and TCG Tavern are the most reliable on Oahu.
  • Singles hunters — chasing specific cards or completing sets. Shops with big singles cases and honest pricing. ToyLynx, Space 62, and Big Island Breaks are strong options.
  • Graded / slab collectors — PSA, CGC, BGS slabs. Several Hawaii shops now offer PSA submission intake; others carry walk-in slab inventory. Maui Sports Cards is a PSA dealer; Big Island Breaks carries graded Pokemon.
  • Japanese import fans — JP booster boxes, Venusaur ex art rares, and other sets that don’t hit the US market. ToyLynx carries the deepest Japanese import inventory in the state.
  • Tournament players — you want a shop with a TCG schedule, prize support, and a competitive community. ToyLynx runs the most consistent tournament calendar on Oahu.

Card Shows with Strong Pokemon Presence

If you’d rather shop dozens of Pokemon vendors in one place, Hawaii’s card show circuit is the fastest way. Every major show carries heavy Pokemon representation:

  • Paradise Card Show — headline Oahu show, draws 1,400+ attendees. Most vendors carry Pokemon.
  • Aloha Card Show — annual Blaisdell Center convention and separate Windward Mall edition.
  • Keep It Aloha Card Show — 40+ TCG vendors, Pokemon-heavy. Next edition July 4–5, 2026.
  • Pokemon Trade Night at The Boba Room — literally Pokemon-focused.
  • Bayview Night Market — Pokemon Collective runs it. 1st and 3rd Tuesdays.
  • Mini Pokemon Market — Pokemon-specific community market.

Full calendar of upcoming shows on the homepage.

Japanese Pokemon in Hawaii: Japanese sets hit Hawaii faster than most mainland markets thanks to direct shipping relationships. ToyLynx is the main source for JP booster boxes and loose product. Some smaller shops also carry Japanese imports — worth asking.

A Few Tips for Buying Pokemon in Hawaii

  • Cash is common. Many smaller shops and show vendors prefer cash. Bring some even if you plan to use cards.
  • Prices compare fairly to the mainland. Sealed product is typically at MSRP or a slight premium. Singles pricing is reasonable — ask politely if you’re paying over TCGplayer.
  • Build relationships. Shop owners remember regulars. Repeat customers get first-access on new hits and fair trade-ins.
  • Follow shops on Instagram. Most Hawaii shops announce drops and restocks on IG before updating their websites.
  • Go to shows. The best singles deals in Hawaii happen vendor-to-customer at live shows, not online.
