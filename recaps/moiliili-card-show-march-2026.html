A First Show That Felt Like a Movement

The Moiliili Card Show made its debut on Saturday, March 28, 2026 — and it didn't just show up, it showed out. Over 1,000 collectors packed into the Moiliili Hongwanji Mission for the venue's very first trading card event, making it one of the strongest inaugural shows Hawaii has seen in recent memory.

Organized by Landon (@just_for_kicks on Instagram), the show brought together 25 vendors and a community that clearly came ready to buy, trade, and connect. From the moment doors opened at 10 AM, the energy was undeniable.

Lines Out the Door — And That Was a Good Thing

The turnout exceeded expectations. Collectors were lined up well before the show opened, and the wait continued throughout the day. Landon came out multiple times to personally apologize to those waiting in line — a class move that spoke to how much he cared about the experience.

But the lines weren't a problem. They were a sign the team behind the show had it figured out. Rather than flooding the venue and creating chaos, Landon and his crew controlled the flow of attendees, keeping the inside manageable and ensuring every collector could actually browse, shop, and enjoy the show. That kind of crowd management is hard to pull off at a first event, and they nailed it.

All proceeds from the Moiliili Card Show go directly to the Moiliili Hongwanji Mission to support community programs and activities. This isn't just a card show — it's the hobby giving back.

Community First

What makes this show stand apart is its purpose. Every dollar raised goes back to the Moiliili Hongwanji itself, funding community activities and supporting the mission that has served the Moiliili neighborhood since 1906. In an era where card shows are big business, the Moiliili Card Show is proof that the hobby's community roots run deep.

The support from the Hongwanji community was evident. This wasn't just a rented space — it was a partnership. The venue felt right, the atmosphere was welcoming, and the whole event carried a spirit that's hard to manufacture: genuine aloha.

What's Next

With a debut this strong, the Moiliili Card Show has put itself on the map. Whether this becomes a recurring event or a one-time legendary first outing, one thing is clear — Landon and the Hongwanji community proved that there's demand, there's heart, and there's room for more shows like this in Hawaii.

Follow @just_for_kicks on Instagram for updates on future shows.
