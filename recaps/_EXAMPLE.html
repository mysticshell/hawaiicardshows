GetNutz x Paradise

For one Sunday evening, the line between Hawaii's streetwear scene and its trading card community didn't exist. GetNutz — one of Hawaii's most recognizable culture brands — teamed up with Paradise Card Show for their first-ever joint event at 819 Valkenburgh St in Honolulu. The result was one of the more unique shows the island has hosted this year: part pop-up, part card show, part neighborhood hangout.

The venue — an open warehouse space — lent itself to the vibe. Vendors lined the walls, music played, and the usual gallery of Pokemon, sports cards, and sealed product was joined by GetNutz apparel, prints, and giveaways. It felt less like a traditional trade floor and more like a cultural cross-pollination moment: collectors in GetNutz tees, streetwear fans flipping through binders for the first time.

The Collab Worked

On paper, pairing a streetwear brand with a card show is a gamble. In practice, the overlap is obvious once you're standing in the room. Both communities are driven by drops, rarity, and community. GetNutz and Paradise's crowd mixed naturally, and by 7 PM the space was packed with collectors who'd normally never cross paths.

GetNutz brought apparel giveaways, limited merch drops, and their signature energy to the floor. The brand has been a quiet but consistent supporter of the local card scene — this was their first time putting their name directly on a show. Based on turnout, it won't be the last. What We Saw

Vendor mix leaned Pokemon and modern sports, with pockets of One Piece, Japanese imports, and sealed wax. A few standouts:

  • A PSA 10 Charizard ex from the 151 set that drew a steady crowd
  • A vintage Jordan rookie binder at one of the sports-focused tables
  • Sealed Obsidian Flames booster boxes priced aggressively for the after-hours crowd

The show also leaned into community moments — a vendor swap happened in the middle of the floor around 6 PM that pulled a small crowd just to watch. That's the kind of thing you don't see at traditional conventions, and it's why hybrid events like this work.

What's Next

Paradise Card Show's next major event is their annual show in July. GetNutz has hinted at more collabs across the hobby space. If you missed this one, the March 2026 Paradise Card Show recap gives a sense of the scale Paradise is capable of at full send.

Mahalo to everyone who came out. The Hawaii card scene keeps finding new ways to grow — this show was a reminder that the community is bigger than any one subculture.
